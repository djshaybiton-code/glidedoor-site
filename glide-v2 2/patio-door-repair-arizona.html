Common Patio Door Problems We Fix

Hard to open or requires two hands — almost always worn rollers. The most common patio door repair in Arizona. Fixed same-day with heat-rated roller assemblies.

Grinding or scraping — either worn rollers dragging on the track, or a damaged track surface. We diagnose on arrival and fix the root cause, not just the symptom.

Door doesn't seal or drafts come through — misaligned door panel, worn weatherstripping, or a warped frame. We re-align the door and replace seals same-day.

Lock won't catch or engage — handle hardware failure or door misalignment preventing the lock from meeting the strike plate. Fixed same-day.

Cracked or shattered glass — tempered safety glass replacement. We measure on-site, order the exact panel, and install within 1–2 business days.

Why Arizona Patio Doors Need More Maintenance

Arizona patio doors handle far more daily use than national averages — pool households may open and close the door 30–50 times per day in summer. That's 3–5x the typical East Coast home.

The UV load in Arizona bleaches and degrades synthetic components — nylon rollers, rubber seals, plastic handle hardware — measurably faster than northern states.

Most Arizona homeowners should plan for patio door roller service every 7–10 years, vs the national average of 12–15 years.

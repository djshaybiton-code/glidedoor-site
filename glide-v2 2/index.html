Diagnose Your Door

Is Your Sliding Door Giving You Trouble?

If your door has felt rougher lately, you're not imagining it. Arizona's heat and desert sand destroy door hardware faster than anywhere else.

🚫 Is this your problem?

  • ✓ Door is hard to open or takes two hands
  • ✓ Grinding or scraping sound when sliding
  • ✓ Door drags, drops, or feels too heavy
  • ✓ Jumps off track or won't slide properly
  • ✓ Rollers feel worn flat — door wobbles
  • ✓ Lock won't engage or latch properly

🔍 What causes it?

  • ✓ Worn or flattened rollers from years of use
  • ✓ Bent, gouged, or corroded bottom track
  • ✓ Door alignment shift as the frame settles
  • ✓ Arizona desert sand packed in the track
  • ✓ Extreme heat degrading roller materials
  • ✓ Damaged or misaligned latch hardware

Not sure what's wrong? We'll diagnose it free — even over the phone.

FAQ

Your Questions, Answered Straight

How fast can you repair my sliding door?
Same-day service 7 days a week across Tempe, Mesa, Scottsdale, Gilbert, and Chandler. Call (646) 221-7897 and we'll schedule you the same day in most cases. Most repairs are completed in 1–2 hours on-site.
Do you back every repair with a warranty?
Yes — every repair comes with a 1-year parts and labor warranty. If anything fails within a year, we return and fix it free. No questions asked, no hassle.
Can you diagnose my door problem over the phone?
Yes. Call (646) 221-7897 and describe what your door is doing. We identify most sliding door problems in under 2 minutes at no charge — before we even set foot in your home.
Should I repair or replace my sliding door?
In the vast majority of cases, repair is the right call and saves significant money. The door frame is almost always sound — it's the hardware that fails. We always recommend repair when viable and tell you honestly when replacement makes better long-term sense. We never upsell replacement.
Do you work on all sliding door brands?
Yes. We service all major brands including Milgard, PGT, Andersen, Pella, JELD-WEN, Weather Shield, Simonton, NanaWall, LaCantina, and all builder-grade brands common in Arizona homes.
Why do Arizona sliding doors fail faster than elsewhere?
Three factors: extreme heat (110–115°F) degrades nylon roller wheels, intense UV breaks down synthetic materials, and fine desert sand constantly abrades rollers and tracks. Arizona doors need service 30–40% sooner than national averages.

Signs Your Track Needs Repair

Your track may be the problem — not the rollers — if you notice: visible bends, dents, or gouges in the metal channel; the door jumps or bounces at specific points when sliding; the door veers to one side instead of tracking straight; deep sand or debris packed solidly into the channel that cleaning doesn't fully resolve.

The clearest sign: if roller replacement didn't fix the problem, the track is the issue. Rollers can't roll smoothly over a damaged surface.

Track Repair vs. Track Replacement

Not every damaged track needs to be replaced. Minor bends and dents can often be straightened with professional tools. We assess each track on-site and recommend the most cost-effective solution.

Track replacement is necessary when: the track has deep gouges that rollers catch on; the track is corroded through; multiple sections are bent; the track has separated from the threshold.

When we replace a track, we remove the door panel, pull out the old channel, install new aluminum track cut to the exact size, and re-seat the door with fresh hardware. Total time: 1.5–2.5 hours.

The Arizona Sand Problem

Desert sand is harder on sliding door tracks than almost any other environmental factor. Fine silica particles pack into the track channel, creating a grinding surface that wears down both rollers and the track itself.

We include a deep track cleaning — compressed air and specialized brushes — with every track service call. We also advise on the right track covers and threshold seals that significantly slow sand accumulation.

Can you repair the track without removing the door?
Minor cleaning and some straightening can be done without full removal. Significant repair or full replacement requires removing the door panel, which we do efficiently without damage.
My track has deep scratches — does it need replacement?
Surface scratches are usually fine. Deep gouges that catch the roller do need to be addressed. We'll assess on-site and show you exactly what we find before recommending anything.
How do I prevent my track from getting damaged again?
Regular track cleaning (every 3–6 months in Arizona), using a track cover or door sweep to reduce sand entry, and not sliding the door when the track has visible sand buildup. We'll show you the right maintenance routine after every service.

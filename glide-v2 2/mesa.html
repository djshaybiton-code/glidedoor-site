Why Mesa Sliding Doors Fail Faster

  • ✓
    Older home hardware
    Mesa has one of the oldest housing stocks in the Valley. Rollers and tracks from the 70s–90s are often past their lifespan.

    Extreme inland heat
    Mesa's inland location means hotter summers. Metal tracks expand more, causing misalignment and roller wear.
  • ✓
    Heavy patio door use
    Mesa's large single-family homes see pool access and family use that creates 4–6x average daily door cycles.

    Fine desert sand
    East Mesa's proximity to open desert means fine silica sand infiltrates tracks constantly, acting as sandpaper on rollers.
